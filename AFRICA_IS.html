My Time in Africa

From 2022 to 2024, I had the opportunity to serve as a full-time missionary for The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints in Abidjan, Côte d'Ivoire — a country on the west coast of Africa. It was two of the most challenging and rewarding years of my life.

Living in Côte d'Ivoire meant fully immersing myself in a new culture, language, and way of life. I quickly learned that the people there have a warmth and resilience that is unlike anything I had experienced before. From the busy streets of Abidjan to small villages with red dirt roads, every day brought something new.

One of the greatest gifts of my mission was becoming fluent in French. I went from knowing almost nothing to teaching, leading meetings, and building deep friendships entirely in French. It is something I am incredibly proud of and plan to use throughout my career.

I served in several leadership roles, mentoring and supervising groups of missionaries, coordinating meetings, and helping others grow. The responsibility pushed me to become a better communicator, a stronger leader, and a more patient person.

The children of Côte d'Ivoire were some of the most joyful people I encountered. Their happiness despite having very little left a lasting impression on me.







What I Learned

  1. Language & Communication
    • Achieved full professional fluency in French
    • Learned to communicate across cultural and language barriers
    • Adapted teaching styles to fit different audiences
  2. Leadership
    • Supervised and mentored 10+ missionaries at a time
    • Coordinated weekly meetings and regional progress reports
    • Handled conflict resolution and team morale in high-pressure situations
  3. Culture & People
    • Lived alongside Ivorian families and communities
    • Learned local customs, greetings, and traditions
    • Built lasting friendships across cultures
  4. Personal Growth
    • Maintained an 80-hour weekly schedule for two years
    • Developed discipline, patience, and resilience
    • Gained a deep appreciation for people and perspectives different from my own
